The "$300 staff" objection — broken down
QuestionReality
Can 1 person handle 5 outlets?Possible if calls spread. But holidays, sick days, peak Friday night — they can't be in 5 places. Tringg handles concurrent calls; 1 human cannot.
True cost at 20 hrs/week$1,300–1,600/month with taxes. Not $300. A $300 hire is ~5 hrs/week — handles maybe 2–3 hrs of peak calls, nothing else.
What a human can't doAnswer at 11pm. Handle 3 simultaneous calls. Auto-transcribe. Send SMS. Provide analytics. Work during their own meal break.
What Tringg must proveYou don't replace a person. You replace missed calls at 10pm, simultaneous Saturday calls, voicemails that never get returned.
Value frame — don't sell "staff replacement"

"Tringg doesn't replace your host. It captures the reservations your host misses — during peak service, after closing, and during staff transitions."

Industry data: 43% of restaurant calls go unanswered. At $50 average spend per cover, 5 missed reservation calls/day = $75,000 revenue/year. Tringg at $249/mo = $2,988/year. The ROI frame writes itself.
